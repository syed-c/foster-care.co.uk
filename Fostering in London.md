## **Why London Continues to Need More Foster Carers**

London’s population changes quickly. Families move, schools change, and life can shift suddenly for many children. As a result, the demand for foster carers remains consistently high across the capital.  
 **London foster care** focuses on providing stability in the middle of a fast-moving city. Carers who can offer routine, patience, and emotional support make an immediate and lasting difference.

Many children benefit from staying close to their borough, near their school, friends, and familiar surroundings, which is why London urgently needs local foster carers in every area.

## **Different Types of Foster Placements Needed in London**

Agencies across the city offer a full range of placements depending on each child’s circumstances.

### **Short-Term Placements**

Care provided during assessments or when decisions about long-term plans are being made.

### **Long-Term Fostering**

Stable homes for children who need consistent care until adulthood.

### **Emergency Placements**

Same-day placements for children in urgent situations.

### **Respite Fostering**

Short stays that support families and carers.

### **Therapeutic Fostering**

Enhanced support for children with emotional or behavioural needs.

### **Parent and Child Placements**

A structured living arrangement helping parents develop confidence and essential skills.

These placement types are available through both independent agencies and local authorities and are listed clearly in our **foster care directory**.

## **Independent or Local Authority Fostering: What Should You Choose?**

Carers in London choose between:

### **Independent Fostering Agencies (IFAs)**

IFAs provide structured support, specialist training, and regular supervision.  
 They often offer 24/7 support lines, therapeutic guidance, and higher allowances depending on placements.

### **Local Authority Fostering**

Each London borough runs its own fostering service.  
 Local authorities focus on keeping placements local, making it easier for children to stay connected to their community, school, and extended family.

Both options support children well; the right choice depends on the type of support and training you prefer.

## **How to Become a Foster Carer in London**

If fostering feels right for you, the next step is understanding the process.  
 The journey to **become a foster carer in London** is structured, supportive, and designed to help you learn at each stage.

### **1\. Initial Conversation**

An agency explains requirements, expectations, and what the role involves.

### **2\. Home Visit**

A social worker meets with you to learn more about your home, lifestyle, and suitability.

### **3\. Training and Assessment**

Preparation training sessions help you understand fostering.  
 A detailed assessment follows, exploring your strengths and ability to support a child.

### **4\. Approval Panel**

Your assessment is reviewed by a fostering panel who make the final decision.

Most families complete approval within four to six months.
